1. Accessing the MathPro Usage Report

Click on MathPro Usage Report. By default, the MathPro Usage – Overall and Unique report will be opened.

2. The KPI Panel

The KPI Panel will be fixed on the top of every page of this report. Based on the filter selection for zone, region, country, state, consultant, school name, grade, curriculum, and date range, the KPI values will change. It contains the following information:

  • Active Students
  • Active Teachers
  • Available Recalls
  • Available Practices
  • Available Assessments
  • Available Reviews

3. MathPro Usage - Overall and Unique

This page contains the following information:

  • Overall Activities: Assigned vs Completed
  • Unique Activities: Assigned vs Completed

3.1 Export and Explore in Excel

Follow these steps to export the graph to Excel:

  1. Click on the three-dot menu at the top of the graph, as shown in the red-highlighted box in the image above.
  2. From the dropdown menu, select "Export Data."

3.2 Which data do you want to export?

  1. In the "Which data do you want to export?" window, under File format, choose .xlsx or .csv.
  2. Click "Export" at the bottom right of the window to complete the process.

3.3 Overall Activities: Assigned vs Completed

This graph provides information on total assigned activities (blue bar) and total completed activities (green bar) year on year across different activity types—Recall, Practice, Assessments, and Reviews. Additionally, the purple line indicates the completion rate, calculated as completed activities divided by assigned activities.

3.4 Unique Activities: Assigned vs Completed

This graph provides information on unique activities assigned (blue bar) and unique activities completed (green bar) across different activity types—Recall, Practice, Assessments, and Reviews—within the selected date range. Additionally, the yellow dot represents the number of unique students who were assigned these activities at least once during the same period.

4. Activity Usage

This page contains the following information:

  • Overall: Activity by Status
  • Unique: Activity by Status

Above the KPI Panel, there is an option to filter the graphs by activity.

4.1 Overall: Activity by Status

This graph presents an overview of activities categorized by their status within the selected date range. The blue bar represents assigned activities, indicating the total number of activities assigned by the teacher. The orange bar denotes activities that have not been started. The peach bar signifies activities in progress, referring to tasks that were assigned at any point and have been started but not yet finished. The green bar highlights completed activities. The grey bar represents re-assigned activities, which were previously completed by students but have been assigned again by the teacher within the chosen timeframe. Finally, purple line indicates the activities completion rate, calculated as the proportion of completed activities to assigned activities.

4.2 Unique: Activity by Status

This graph provides information on unique activities by their status for the selected date range. Activities assigned, denoted by the blue bar, represent the distinct activities assigned by the teacher. Activities not started, indicated by the red bar, include assigned activities that remain untouched by students. Activities in progress, represented by the yellow bar, refer to activities that have been started but not yet completed. The green bar highlights completed activities. Activities re-assigned, depicted by the grey bar, represent activities that were previously completed but have been assigned again by the teacher. The purple line indicates the activities completion rate, calculated again in the same manner.

5. Average Usage

This page contains the following information:

  • Average Activities Completed

5.1 Average Activities Completed

This graph displays the average number of activities for each category: Overall, Recall, Practice, Assessments, and Review. The colours represent each category as follows: Overall is denoted by purple, Recall by yellow, Practice by blue, Assessments by red, and Review by light blue.
